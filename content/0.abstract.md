<!-- Context: Why the need is so pressing or important -->
SPARQL engines continue to diverge in the versions and language extensions they support.
With SPARQL 1.2 nearing standardisation and moving toward maintenance mode, this divergence is likely to grow.
<!-- Need: Why something needed to be done at all -->
As a result, language tools that aim to support multiple SPARQL versions and dialects face increasing maintenance costs, and federated querying becomes harder to manage.
<!-- Task: What was undertaken to address the need -->
To address these challenges, we introduce Traqula, a modular framework for parsing, generating, and transforming SPARQL queries.
<!-- Object: What the present document does or covers -->
This paper presents Traqula’s architecture and its builder-based inversion-of-control design,
showing how these components provide modular support for SPARQL 1.1 and 1.2 parsing and generation.
<!-- Findings: What the work done yielded or revealed -->
Traqula enables round-tripping, supports generic algebra and AST transformations,
and delivers significantly faster parsing than the existing JavaScript-based parser.
<!-- Conclusion: What the findings mean for the audience -->
Its modular structure simplifies maintenance and experimentation while offering reliable tooling for heterogeneous query environments.
<!-- Perspectives: What the future holds, beyond this work -->
Looking forward, Traqula lays the groundwork for supporting additional query languages,
developing generic rewriting modules for federation engines, and building robust tooling for an increasingly diverse SPARQL ecosystem.
